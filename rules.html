Rules of Procedure

UNGA DISEC

Organized by the Debate & Quiz Club | Gati Shakti Vishwavidyalaya | EPITOME 2026

I. Opening Procedures

1. Roll Call

At the beginning of each committee session, the Chair will conduct a Roll Call by calling the name of each country represented in the committee. Delegates may respond with either:

Present

The delegate is present and may vote In Favour / Against / Abstain during substantive voting.

Present and Voting

The delegate is present and must vote either In Favour or Against during substantive voting and may not abstain.

Note: Delegates responding "Present and Voting" may not change this status later during the session.

2. Agenda Introduction

The committee will begin discussions on the agenda set for the committee. The Chair may briefly introduce the agenda and invite delegates to begin debate.

II. Modes of Debate

3. General Speakers List (GSL)

The General Speakers List (GSL) is the default format of debate. Delegates wishing to speak may raise their placard or request to be added to the speakers list.

  • Typical speaking time: 60–90 seconds
  • Delegates may yield remaining speaking time to another delegate, questions from the committee, or the Chair.

The GSL continues unless interrupted by motions such as caucuses.

4. Moderated Caucus

Delegates may motion for a Moderated Caucus to discuss a specific subtopic within the agenda.

Example Motion:
"Motion for a 10-minute moderated caucus with 45 seconds speaking time per delegate on the topic of [subtopic]."
  • Typical duration: 8–12 minutes
  • Speaking time per delegate: 30–60 seconds
  • The Chair recognizes speakers. Discussion must remain relevant to the proposed topic.

4. Moderated Caucus

Delegates may motion for a Moderated Caucus to discuss a specific subtopic within the agenda.

Example Motion:
"Motion for a 10-minute moderated caucus with 45 seconds speaking time per delegate on the topic of [subtopic]."
  • The Chair recognizes speakers.
  • Delegates speak for the allotted time.
  • Discussion must remain relevant to the proposed topic.

5. Unmoderated Caucus

An Unmoderated Caucus allows delegates to freely interact with each other. Delegates may move around the room and collaborate with others.

  • Typical duration: 10–20 minutes
  • Used for informal negotiations, formation of blocs, and drafting working papers or draft resolutions.
Chair's Discretion: The Chair reserves the right to adjust speaking times and caucus durations in the interest of productive debate.

III. Parliamentary Points

6. Points

Delegates may raise points to address procedural matters.

Point of Personal Privilege

Raised when a delegate experiences personal discomfort that affects their ability to participate (e.g., inability to hear the speaker, room temperature issues). This point may interrupt a speaker only when absolutely necessary.

Point of Parliamentary Inquiry

Used to ask the Chair questions regarding the rules of procedure.

Example: "Point of Parliamentary Inquiry: Could the Chair clarify the voting procedure?"

Point of Order

Raised when a delegate believes that the rules of procedure are not being properly followed. The Chair will rule on the point.

Right of Reply

A delegate may request a Right of Reply if their country has been directly insulted or misrepresented during debate. Granting a Right of Reply is at the discretion of the Chair.

IV. The Resolution Process

7. Draft Resolutions

Delegates collaborate to draft resolutions, which propose solutions to the agenda. Delegates typically work in groups or blocs based on similar policy positions (e.g., Western bloc, Eastern bloc, Neutral bloc).

8. Sponsors and Signatories

A draft resolution requires two types of supporting delegates:

Sponsors (Min: 2, Max: 5)

Sponsors are the main authors of the resolution and fully support its content.

Signatories

Delegates who support introducing the resolution for debate. They do not necessarily support the content. Signatories contribute toward the minimum signature requirement (20%) for submission.

9. Submission of Draft Resolution

  • It must have signatures from at least 20% of the committee members.
  • It must be approved by the Chair before being introduced.

Once approved, the resolution may be presented to the committee for debate.

V. Voting Procedure

10. Closing Debate

A delegate may motion to close debate once sufficient discussion has taken place. If the motion passes, the committee will move into voting procedure.

11. Voting Procedure

During voting procedure, the chamber will be sealed. Delegates may not enter or leave, and no talking is permitted. Voting options are:

  • In Favour
  • Against
  • Abstain (only permitted for delegates who declared "Present")
  • Delegates may not use electronic devices during voting procedure.

12. Passing of Resolutions

A draft resolution passes if votes in favour exceed votes against. Abstentions are not counted in the final tally. If the resolution fails, the committee may proceed to vote on other draft resolutions.

VI. Delegate Guidelines & Awards

13. Recognition

Outstanding performance in committee may be formally recognized by the Executive Board at the conclusion of the conference. All participants will receive Certificates of Participation. Any additional recognitions or awards, if applicable, will be announced soon, subject to the decision by College Administration.

14. Conduct of Delegates

Delegates are expected to maintain diplomatic conduct, respectful debate, adherence to the rules of procedure, and cooperation with the Executive Board. Disruptive behavior may result in disciplinary action at the discretion of the Chair and the Organising Committee.

15. Preparation for Delegates

Delegates are encouraged to:

  • Research their assigned country extensively.
  • Understand their country’s foreign policy deeply.
  • Prepare arguments and formulate possible solutions prior to the conference.
  • Actively participate in committee debate.

VII. General Instructions for Delegates

1. Language of Communication

English shall be the official and default mode of communication throughout the conference. Delegates are expected to conduct speeches, motions, and interactions primarily in English.

2. Limited Use of Hindi

Delegates may use up to ten Hindi words during a speech without penalty. Excessive use of Hindi beyond this limit may result in procedural penalties at the discretion of the Executive Board.

3. Use of Research Material

Delegates are permitted to bring printed research reports, notes, or briefing papers related to the agenda. These materials may be referred to during speeches or discussions within the committee.

4. Code of Conduct

Delegates must maintain professional and respectful conduct at all times. The use of abusive language, personal attacks, or discriminatory remarks will not be tolerated and may lead to immediate disqualification.

5. Authority of the Organising Committee

The Organising Committee reserves the full authority to amend any rules or procedures whenever deemed necessary. All decisions taken by the Organising Committee and the Executive Board shall be final and binding.

6. Respect for the Executive Board

Delegates are expected to follow the instructions of the Chair and Vice-Chair at all times. Decisions made by the Executive Board regarding procedural matters must be respected.

7. Use of Electronic Devices

Mobile phones and laptops may be used only for research purposes during committee sessions and must not disrupt proceedings.

8. Diplomatic Conduct

Delegates must maintain diplomatic decorum throughout the conference. All remarks must be addressed through the Chair and not directly toward other delegates.

9. Attendance and Participation

Delegates are expected to remain present and actively participate during committee sessions unless granted permission by the Executive Board to leave temporarily.

10. Committee Decorum

Delegates may only speak when recognized by the Chair. Interrupting speakers, shouting across the committee, or engaging in direct arguments without recognition is strictly prohibited.

11. Respectful Diplomatic Language

Delegates must maintain respectful and professional language during debate. Personal attacks or offensive remarks toward any country, delegate, or community will not be tolerated.

12. Academic Integrity

Delegates are encouraged to present their own research and arguments. While research materials may be referenced, speeches should reflect the delegate’s understanding of the topic.

13. Active Participation

Delegates are encouraged to actively participate through speeches, motions, and drafting resolutions. Engagement and quality of participation may be considered when recognizing outstanding delegates.

14. Time Management

Delegates must adhere to the time limits set by the Executive Board for speeches, caucuses, and procedural motions. The Chair reserves the right to enforce time limits strictly.

15. Interpretation of Rules

In situations not explicitly covered by the Rules of Procedure or these instructions, the interpretation and decision of the Executive Board shall prevail.
